Accurately tracking work hours is essential for payroll. This tool acts as a digital time card for employers and employees.

How to Use

  • Enter your Start and End times.
  • Supports 24-hour format inputs.
  • Enter minutes in 'Break Deduction' to subtract unpaid breaks.

History of the 8-Hour Workday

The 8-hour workday originated in 16th-century Spain. Modern movements trace back to the Industrial Revolution in the UK. In 1817, Robert Owen coined the slogan '8 hours labor, 8 hours recreation, 8 hours rest'. The massive strikes in the US on May 1, 1886, solidified this movement.

Work Arrangements
  • Standard: 9-to-5, 40 hours/week.
  • Flexitime: Allows flexible start/end times.
  • Compressed Week: 4 days x 10 hours.
  • Shift Work: 24/7 operations, rotating schedules.
  • Freelance: Result-based, flexible hours.
