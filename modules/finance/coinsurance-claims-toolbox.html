Understanding Coinsurance

Coinsurance clauses require policyholders to maintain insurance coverage of at least a specified percentage of the property's actual cash valueâ€”typically 80%, 90%, or 100%.

This ensures adequate coverage for potential losses. Failure to meet this requirement may result in a "Coinsurance Penalty" where the policyholder shares the loss.

Key Terms

  • A (Amount Payable): Final claim payment amount.
  • C (Carried): Amount of insurance actually purchased (Did).
  • R (Required): Amount of insurance required (Should) = Value Ã— %.
  • Penalty: Reduction in payment due to underinsurance. Ratio C/R is capped at 1.

About Depreciation

Depreciation is the reduction in value of an asset over time due to wear and tear. This tool provides field-verified estimates for insurance professionals.

Unlike accounting methods, this reflects actual value loss for fair indemnity based on item age and type.

Formulas & Variables

  • ACV: Actual Cash Value = RCV - Depreciation.
  • RCV: Replacement Cost Value (Cost New).
  • AGE: Usage duration in years.
  • DPR: Annual depreciation rate percentage.
  • Note: Data sourced from manufacturers and insurers. Averages used.
